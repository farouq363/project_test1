Geography of Palestine

Discover the diverse landscapes, location, and natural beauty of Palestine.

Key Geographical Information

Location

Palestine is situated in the Middle East, at the crossroads of Africa, Asia, and Europe. It lies along the eastern Mediterranean coast, bordering Israel to the north and west, Jordan to the east, and Egypt to the southwest.

Size and Borders

Palestine covers an area of approximately 6,020 square kilometers. It includes two main regions:

  • West Bank: A landlocked territory west of the Jordan River.
  • Gaza Strip: A coastal region along the Mediterranean Sea.

Its borders have been shaped by historical agreements and conflicts, and they remain a significant topic in geopolitical discussions.

Natural Features

Palestine boasts a variety of landscapes, from rolling hills and fertile valleys to arid deserts. Key features include:

  • Jordan River: A historic and religiously significant river.
  • Dead Sea: The lowest point on Earth and a major tourist attraction.
  • Mountains: The Judean and Samarian hills offer breathtaking views.
  • Coastal Areas: The Gaza Strip features sandy beaches along the Mediterranean Sea.

Climate

Palestine has a Mediterranean climate characterized by hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. The coastal regions experience more moderate weather, while the inland areas can see more extreme temperatures.
